Thailand

Grand Palace

The Grand Palace in Bangkok is a stunning complex of buildings that served as the royal residence for over 150 years. Its intricate architecture and the Emerald Buddha Temple within the grounds are spectacular. Visitors can explore the ornate halls, gardens, and learn about the rich history of Thailand’s monarchy.

Thailand

Chatuchak Weekend Market

One of the largest markets in the world, Chatuchak offers over 15,000 stalls selling everything from clothing to vintage items and local handicrafts. It's a haven for shoppers and food lovers alike, where you can enjoy a variety of delicious Thai street food while exploring the vibrant market atmosphere.

Thailand

Wat Pho

Home to the famous Reclining Buddha, Wat Pho is one of Bangkok's oldest temples and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its stunning architecture features intricate murals and statues. The temple is also known for its traditional Thai massage school, where visitors can learn about this ancient healing practice.

Thailand

Ayutthaya Historical Park

A UNESCO World Heritage site, Ayutthaya Historical Park contains the ruins of temples and palaces from the ancient capital of the Kingdom of Siam. Visitors can explore the impressive archaeological sites, including Wat Phra Si Sanphet and Wat Mahathat, and learn about the rich history of Thailand.

Railay Beach

The former Police Station cum Victoria Prison has been renovated to its former glory extended with two new building volumes. It’s amazing courtyard space is now open to the public. As are the former prison cells. The building further houses shops, restaurants and exhibition venues.
