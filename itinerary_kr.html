Gyeongbokgung Palace

Gyeongbokgung is the largest and most iconic of the Five Grand Palaces in Seoul. Built in 1395, it showcases traditional Korean architecture and beautiful gardens. Bukchon Hanok Village

This picturesque village features hundreds of traditional hanoks (Korean houses) that date back to the Joseon Dynasty. Visitors can stroll through its narrow alleyways, experience traditional tea houses, and enjoy panoramic views of both the village and the modern city skyline, making it a perfect blend of history and culture.

Myeongdong Shopping Street

Myeongdong is a bustling shopping district known for its vibrant atmosphere, filled with shops selling fashion, cosmetics, and street food. Visitors can sample delicious Korean snacks like tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes) and hotteok (sweet pancakes) while enjoying the lively street performances and neon lights that characterize this area.

Namsan Tower

Namsan Tower, also known as Seoul Tower, offers breathtaking views of the city from its observation decks. Visitors can hike up Namsan Mountain or take a cable car to reach the tower. It's a popular spot for couples to attach love locks and is beautifully illuminated at night, making it a romantic destination.

Korea

Insa-dong

Insa-dong is a cultural district known for its traditional tea houses, antique shops, and art galleries. Visitors can immerse themselves in Korean culture by trying traditional teas, purchasing handmade crafts, and enjoying street performances. The area is particularly lively during festivals and is a great place to experience the artistic side of Korea.
